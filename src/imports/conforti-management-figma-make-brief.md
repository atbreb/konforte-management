# Conforti Management — Website Design Brief for Figma Make

**Client:** Conforti Management  
**Type:** Property Management Company (3 Retail Shopping Centers, Central Florida)  
**Goal:** Professional web presence that makes a boutique 3-property portfolio feel established and trustworthy  
**Design Tone:** Professional but approachable — not cold corporate. Clean, confident, community-rooted.

---

## Inspiration Sites

### Primary: [SiteCenters.com](https://www.sitecenters.com/)
SITE Centers is a publicly traded REIT managing open-air retail shopping centers across the U.S. Their site excels at presenting commercial retail properties in a polished, institutional-grade layout. Key patterns to borrow:

- **Homepage hero:** Large full-bleed property photography with bold headline overlay ("PREMIER Retail REAL ESTATE") and a featured property callout. Minimal text, maximum visual impact.
- **Strategy storytelling section:** Below the hero, SiteCenters uses an interactive hotspot image with three pillars (Wealthiest Suburbs / Convenience / Last-Mile Bridge) — each with a short descriptor. Adapt this as a "Why Conforti" value proposition section with 3 differentiators.
- **Focused Approach section:** A text-and-image split layout with a brief paragraph explaining their positioning, followed by a prominent "Explore Our Properties" CTA button. Clean, one-column reading flow.
- **Properties listing page:** Filterable table layout with columns for Property Name + City, Size (sq ft), Available Units, and Major Tenants. Above the table sits a map placeholder and filter bar with dropdowns (State, City, Zip, Market, Unit Size, Property Size). For Conforti's 3 properties, adapt this as a card grid rather than a table.
- **Individual property detail page:** Opens with a property description paragraph, then address + downloadable flyer/site plan links. Followed by a "Trade Area" stats bar (Population, Households, Avg HH Income within a 10-minute drive). Then an "Available Units" table listing each retail unit with its square footage and a per-unit "Email" contact action. Then a "Featured Retailers" list showing existing tenants. Then a "Contact Us" section with named contacts (Leasing, Property Manager) including phone and embedded email form.
- **Footer:** Multi-column footer with grouped links (company, investor relations, tenants) plus copyright and legal links.
- **Navigation:** Minimal top nav — only 4 items. Sticky header with logo left, nav links right.

### Secondary: [V3CapGroup.com](https://v3capgroup.com/)
V3 Capital Group is a Central Florida boutique real estate firm operating out of Apopka and Melbourne — direct local competitor and the client's original benchmark. Key patterns to borrow:

- **Animated counter stats:** Homepage prominently displays large animated stat counters — Commercial Square Feet Managed, Value of Current Assets, Portfolio Occupancy %. These serve as instant trust indicators. Conforti should have similar stats (e.g., Total Sq Ft Managed, Number of Tenants, Occupancy Rate, Properties Managed).
- **Services grid with icons:** V3 presents each service (Property Management, Brokerage, Development, Capital Procurement, Asset Acquisition) as a card with an icon, short headline, and brief description. Each links to a dedicated service detail page.
- **Team bios with professional photos:** Dedicated advisors/team page with circular or rectangular headshots, full name, title, and multi-paragraph bios covering education, experience, and community involvement. Bio format: name, title, then narrative paragraphs.
- **Testimonials:** Client testimonials with attribution (full name, title, company). Used on the Property Management service page as a trust element.
- **Property listings page:** A portfolio page showing managed properties. Less detailed than SiteCenters but appropriate for a smaller portfolio.
- **Local Central Florida identity:** Locations in Apopka and Melbourne prominently displayed. Geographic focus is a selling point, not a limitation.
- **Contact bar in footer:** Dual-location addresses (Orlando area + Melbourne area), email, phone, social follow links.

## Pages & Features

### Page 1: Homepage

The homepage is the front door. It must immediately communicate professionalism, establish Conforti as a credible property management company, and route visitors to properties or contact.

**Section 1 — Hero**
- Full-width hero image: aerial or exterior shot of one of Conforti's retail shopping centers
- Headline overlay: Company tagline (e.g., "Professional Retail Property Management in Central Florida")
- Subheadline: One sentence positioning statement
- Primary CTA button: "View Our Properties"
- Secondary CTA button: "Contact Us"
- *Inspiration: SiteCenters hero with bold typography over property photography; V3's clean headline approach*

**Section 2 — Trust Indicators (Stats Bar)**
- Horizontal bar with 3–4 animated counter stats
- Suggested metrics: Total Square Feet Managed | Number of Tenants | Properties Managed | Occupancy Rate (%)
- Clean, large numbers with labels below
- *Inspiration: V3 Capital Group animated stat counters*

**Section 3 — Featured Properties**
- 3-column card grid, one card per shopping center
- Each card: property hero photo, property name, city/address, total square footage, number of available spaces, "View Details" link
- Section heading: "Our Properties" or "Explore Our Portfolio"
- *Inspiration: SiteCenters properties listing adapted to card format for a smaller portfolio*

**Section 4 — About / Company Overview**
- Split layout: text block on the left, team photo or property collage on the right
- Brief paragraph about who Conforti Management is, their owner-operator advantage, and Central Florida focus
- CTA: "Learn More About Us"
- *Inspiration: SiteCenters "A Focused Approach" text-image split section*

**Section 5 — Services Overview**
- 3–4 service cards in a grid row
- Each card: icon, service name, 1–2 sentence description
- Services to include: Property Management, Tenant Relations, Lease Administration, Maintenance & Operations
- CTA linking each card to the full Services page
- *Inspiration: V3 Capital services grid with icons and short descriptions*

**Section 6 — Call to Action Banner**
- Full-width colored banner with a heading like "Looking for Retail Space in Central Florida?"
- Brief line of text + "Contact Us Today" button
- Contrasting background color to break the page rhythm

**Section 7 — Footer**
- Multi-column layout
- Column 1: Conforti logo + brief tagline + office address
- Column 2: Quick links (Home, Properties, About, Services, FAQ, Blog, Contact)
- Column 3: Contact info (phone, email) + social media icons
- Bottom bar: copyright notice, privacy policy link, terms of use link
- *Inspiration: SiteCenters multi-column footer structure; V3 dual-location footer*

### Page 2: Properties (Portfolio Listing)

This page lists all 3 retail shopping centers with enough detail for prospective tenants to identify opportunities.

**Section 1 — Page Header**
- Page title: "Our Properties"
- Subtitle: "Explore available retail spaces across our Central Florida portfolio"
- Optional: simple filter/sort controls (by availability, by square footage) — but with only 3 properties, filtering is optional. Include it as a design element for future scalability.
- *Inspiration: SiteCenters "Explore Our Properties" page header with filter bar*

**Section 2 — Properties Grid**
- 3 property cards (or 1-column large cards for more detail per card)
- Each card includes:
  - Hero photo (exterior shot)
  - Property name
  - Full address
  - Total square footage
  - Number of available spaces (badge or tag, e.g., "3 Spaces Available")
  - Property type badge: "Retail Shopping Center"
  - "View Property Details" CTA button
- *Inspiration: SiteCenters property table adapted to a visual card grid with photography*

**Section 3 — Map**
- Embedded Google Map showing all 3 property locations with pins
- Interactive pins that link to each property's detail page
- *Inspiration: SiteCenters map placeholder on the properties listing page*

**Section 4 — Leasing CTA**
- "Interested in leasing retail space?" + contact form link or phone number
- Full-width banner similar to homepage CTA section

### Page 3: Individual Property Detail (Template — one per property)

Each of the 3 properties gets its own detail page following the same template. This is where prospective tenants get all the information they need.

**Section 1 — Property Hero**
- Full-width property photo (exterior or aerial)
- Property name as large heading overlay
- Address below the heading

**Section 2 — Property Description**
- 1–2 paragraph description of the property: location context, surrounding area, traffic patterns, anchor tenants, community it serves
- Sidebar or inline links: downloadable property flyer (PDF), site plan (PDF)
- *Inspiration: SiteCenters property detail page opening description + flyer/site plan links*

**Section 3 — Trade Area / Key Stats**
- Horizontal stat bar (similar to SiteCenters "Trade Area" section)
- Metrics: Total Property Size (sq ft) | Available Space (sq ft) | Number of Units | Occupancy Rate
- Optional additional stats: Population within X-mile radius, Average Household Income in trade area
- *Inspiration: SiteCenters trade area stats (Population, Households, Avg HH Income)*

**Section 4 — Photo Gallery**
- Grid or carousel of property photos: exterior angles, interior common areas, aerial view, signage, parking
- Lightbox functionality on click

**Section 5 — Available Spaces**
- Table or card list of individual available units
- Each row/card: Unit number or suite, square footage, unit type (inline, endcap, pad site), lease rate or "Contact for Pricing"
- Inline "Inquire" CTA per unit that links to the contact form pre-filled with the property and unit info
- *Inspiration: SiteCenters "Available Units" table with per-unit email contact*

**Section 6 — Current Tenants**
- Grid of tenant logos or a simple list of current tenant names
- Demonstrates the property's tenant mix quality and co-tenancy
- *Inspiration: SiteCenters "Featured Retailers" list*

**Section 7 — Map Embed**
- Google Maps embed centered on this specific property
- Showing surrounding context (roads, nearby businesses)

**Section 8 — Contact / Leasing Inquiry**
- Named contact: Leasing contact name, phone number, email
- Embedded contact form (Name, Email, Phone, Company, Message, pre-selected property)
- *Inspiration: SiteCenters per-property contact section with named Leasing and Property Manager contacts*

### Page 4: About

Establishes credibility and introduces the team. Makes a small company feel established.

**Section 1 — Page Header**
- Page title: "About Conforti Management"
- Subtitle: brief mission statement or tagline

**Section 2 — Company Story**
- 2–3 paragraphs telling the Conforti Management story: who they are, why they formed their own management company, their owner-operator philosophy, Central Florida roots
- Split layout: text alongside a team photo or office/property image
- Key messaging: "As property owners ourselves, we bring an owner's perspective to every management decision."
- *Inspiration: V3's "As owners ourselves, we understand what it takes" positioning*

**Section 3 — Mission / Values**
- 3 value cards in a row, each with an icon, value name, and 1-sentence description
- Example values: Owner's Perspective, Tenant-First Service, Local Expertise
- *Inspiration: SiteCenters three-pillar section (Wealthiest Suburbs / Convenience / Last-Mile Bridge) adapted to company values*

**Section 4 — Team Members**
- Team member cards in a grid
- Each card: professional headshot photo, full name, title/role, brief bio (2–3 sentences)
- Team members: Justin (Founder/Principal), Yoram (Partner), Property Manager, Accountant (if public-facing)
- *Inspiration: V3 team page with detailed professional bios and headshots*

**Section 5 — Stats / By the Numbers**
- Repeat or variation of the trust indicator stats from the homepage
- Reinforces credibility on the About page

Details the property management services Conforti offers. Important for when they expand to manage properties for other owners.

**Section 1 — Page Header**

- Subtitle: "Comprehensive property management for Central Florida retail centers"

- A visual timeline or numbered step flow showing how Conforti's management engagement works:
  - Step 1: Initial Consultation
  - Step 2: Property Assessment
  - Step 3: Management Plan
  - Step 4: Ongoing Operations & Reporting
- Clean horizontal or vertical timeline graphic

**Section 4 — CTA**
- "Ready to discuss your property?" + contact button

### Page 6: FAQ

Answers common questions from prospective tenants. Reduces friction in the leasing process.

**Section 1 — Page Header**
- Page title: "Frequently Asked Questions"
- Subtitle: "Answers to common questions from prospective and current tenants"

**Section 2 — FAQ Accordion**
- Expandable accordion-style Q&A blocks, organized by category
- **Leasing Questions:**
  - What types of retail spaces are available?
  - What are typical lease terms?
  - What is the leasing process?
  - Are tenant improvements (TI) available?
  - Can I see a space before applying?
- **Costs & Fees:**
  - What are CAM (Common Area Maintenance) charges?
  - What is included in my lease rate?
  - Are utilities included?
  - What is the security deposit requirement?
- **Operations & Maintenance:**
  - How do I submit a maintenance request?
  - What are the property operating hours?
  - Is there on-site management?
  - What is the parking situation?
- **General:**
  - Where are your properties located?
  - Who do I contact for leasing inquiries?
  - Do you manage properties for other owners?

**Section 3 — Still Have Questions CTA**
- "Can't find what you're looking for?" + contact form link

## Global Elements (Present on Every Page)

### Header / Navigation
- Sticky top navigation bar
- Logo left-aligned
- Navigation links right-aligned: Home, Properties, About, Services, FAQ, Blog, Contact
- Mobile: hamburger menu with slide-out drawer
- "Contact Us" styled as a CTA button in the nav (accent color)
- *Inspiration: SiteCenters minimal 4-item nav; expand to 7 for Conforti's full page set*

### Footer
- Consistent multi-column footer (described in Homepage Section 7)
- Present on every page with identical layout

### Mobile Responsiveness
- All pages must be mobile-first responsive
- Property cards stack to single column on mobile
- Navigation collapses to hamburger
- Contact accessibility: phone number is tap-to-call on mobile
- Stats bar wraps to 2x2 grid on mobile
- *Note: V3's site has limited mobile optimization — this is a differentiation opportunity*

## Design Direction Summary

| Attribute | Direction |
|-----------|-----------|
| **Overall tone** | Professional, approachable, community-rooted — not cold institutional |
| **Color palette** | Draw from SiteCenters' clean navy/white/grey palette as a base; add a warm accent color (teal, copper, or warm gold) to differentiate from sterile corporate looks |
| **Typography** | Clean sans-serif (similar to SiteCenters). Bold headings, light body text. Strong hierarchy. |
| **Photography** | Property photography is hero content. Large, high-quality exterior and aerial shots dominate. |
| **Layout** | Generous whitespace. Full-width sections. Card-based property displays. |
| **Trust signals** | Stats bar on homepage and about page. Named contacts with photos. Client testimonials if available. |
| **CTAs** | Contact-oriented CTAs on every page. Every property card and detail page drives toward inquiry. |
| **Mobile** | Mobile-first. Tap-to-call. Stacked layouts. Touch-friendly navigation. |
